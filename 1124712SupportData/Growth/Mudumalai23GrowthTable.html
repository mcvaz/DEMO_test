Growth data from a Center for Tropical Forest Science census plot. When in quotes and not italicized, the species name indicates a morphospecies, or a taxon that we recognize consistently but have not matched to a known herbarium specimen. Otherwise, genus plus species provide a valid botanical name. Question marks and the codes cf. and aff. indicate doubt, and those names were given quotes. Infraspecific names are concatenated to the species name to ensure correct downloading into a spreadsheet. The taxonomic family for each genus can be looked up at http://ctfs.si.edu/apgnames. N: number of individuals; time: mean time interval (years) between the two censuses for the N individuals; rate: observed relative growth rate (mean of log-transformed relative growth rates of individual trees, subsequently back-transformed); fitted: estimated growth rate from Gibbs sampler; lower and upper: 95 pct. confidence limits of the fitted rate, based on the Gibbs sampler (see Methods in Supporting Online Material). All rates are percentages, that is, 100 times the relative growth. _10 indicates data for the 10-49-mm dbh category, _50 for stems 50-99 mm, and _100 for stems dbh 100 and above.
